Bach Phrase Shapes
Visual Reference

Seven melodic contour patterns rooted in Bach's voice-leading logic. Each one is a reusable building block for composing melodies that sound inevitable.

The core pattern: stepwise ascent then drop — earn the leap by stepping into it. All seven shapes below are variations and extensions of this fundamental principle. Steps build connection; leaps create drama. The ratio between them is what gives a melody its character.
01
Step Up, Leap Down

The seed motif. Walk up by stepwise motion (seconds), then leap down a fourth or fifth. The steps build expectation, the leap releases it. Three steps earns one leap.

D → E → F  then  ↓ A
02
Leap Up, Step Down

The reverse. Jump up a fifth or sixth to grab attention, then walk back down by steps to resolve the tension. The leap creates drama, the descent restores smoothness. Bach uses this to cover wide range without losing lyrical quality.

A4 → F5 ↑  then  E5 → D5 → C5
03
The Arch

Step up to a peak, then step back down past where you started. The symmetry gives it completeness, and dropping below the origin creates forward momentum into the next phrase. The peak is the emotional center — everything builds toward it and falls away.

D → E → F ★ peak → E → D → C (below origin)
04
Implied Polyphony

Alternate between high and low registers. Your ear separates the stream into two independent lines — a rising upper voice and a falling lower voice. One melody becomes two. This is the core of Bach's cello suite writing.

upper voice (rising) lower voice (falling)
D5 → A3 → E5 → G3 → F5 → F3
05
The Circle

Arpeggiate up through one chord, then step down to the root of the next chord and arpeggiate up again. Each arpeggio "bloom" connects to the next by a smooth stepwise link. This is how Bach makes single lines imply full chord progressions.

D → F → A  ↓ G  → B♭ → D   (repeat for each chord)
06
The Sigh

Hold a note from one beat into the next where it becomes dissonant, then drop by step to resolve. String several together descending and you get that weeping, inevitable Bach character. Each resolution becomes the next suspension.

hold → clash → resolve ↓ → hold → clash → resolve ↓ → …
07
Fortspinnung Chain

Take the step-step-step-drop shape and use the landing note as the start of the next iteration. Each phrase hands off to the next — nothing repeats exactly, but the shape recurs and the whole thing spirals forward. The tail becomes the head.

D→E→F→↓A → A→B→C→↓E → E→F→G→↓ …
The landing note of each phrase becomes the launching note of the next. Nothing repeats exactly, but the contour recurs — the whole melody spirals forward with a sense of inevitability. This is the most directly useful extension of the step-step-step-drop shape.
